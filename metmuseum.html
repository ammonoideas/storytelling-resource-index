THE MET MUSEUM COLLECTION

The Metropolitan Museum of Art has an extensive digital archive of all the objects in their collection. You can find illustrations, clothing, tools, and more all the way from 8000 B.C. to the present day.

I like to use the Met's collection as a source of reference for fantasy and historically inspired visual development. It's helpful for designing outfits and props based on a particular setting in history. I find that museum collections tend to be more useful as sources of reference than Google or Pinterest because of their specificity of purpose and the context that comes with each object: you can know for sure that an object has been verified as being from, say, the 1920s, rather than having to take the word of whoever uploaded that image to the Internet.
